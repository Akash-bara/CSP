Food Adulteration Survey

A Case Study at Sri Chaitanya Jr College & Local Households

Introduction

Food adulteration is a growing concern in our society. To understand its prevalence and raise awareness, our team conducted a detailed survey at Sri Chaitanya Jr College and nearby households. This case study documents our findings, methodology, and observations.

Objectives

  • Identify common adulterated food items consumed by students and households.
  • Educate people about simple tests to detect adulteration.
  • Create awareness about the health risks of adulterated food.

Methodology

Our survey was conducted in two phases:

  1. Phase 1 – College Awareness: We visited classrooms and demonstrated simple tests for milk, salt, turmeric, and other items.
  2. Phase 2 – Household Survey: We visited nearby homes, collected samples, and explained methods to check adulteration.

Our Team

Classroom Demonstrations

We actively interacted with students, explained the dangers of food adulteration, and demonstrated simple tests to help them identify adulterated food items.

Awareness Session with Principal

Our team met with the Principal of Sri Chaitanya Jr College to discuss the findings of our survey and create awareness at an administrative level.

Household Visits

Observations

During our survey, we discovered that:

  • Many students were unaware of simple adulteration tests.
  • Several households regularly consumed untested milk from local vendors.
  • After our demonstrations, people showed great interest in learning about safe food practices.

Conclusion

This survey helped spread awareness about food adulteration and empowered people to take precautions. We observed a positive response from both students and households, with many promising to use these simple tests regularly.
